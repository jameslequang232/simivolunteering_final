Simi Volunteering - Current Volunteer Opportunities
Website: https://simivolunteering.netlify.app/

1. Kaashi Foundation
   Duties: Help out wherever needed. Open Tues, Wed, Fri, Sat from 4:30–6:30 PM. Arrive 15 min early to help set up.
   Qualifications: None required. Open to all volunteers willing to help.
   Website: https://www.kaashifoundation.org/

2. Simi Valley Youth Council
   Duties: The Simi Valley Youth Council is an advisory board to the City Council on issues affecting youth in the community. Composed of 21 members and up to three alternates, representing every high school and middle school in Simi Valley, as well as the Community at Large.
   Qualifications: Must be in 7th–12th grade in the next school year.
   Website: https://www.simivalley.org/departments/city-manager-s-office/community-programs-and-facilities/youth-council

3. Teen Advisory Group
   Duties: Members help shape the library's teen program by planning events, selecting books and movies for the collection, creating displays, decorating the Teen Lounge, and reviewing books.
   Qualifications: Open to teens entering the 7th through 12th grades who are interested in making their voices heard. Application can be found on their website.
   Website: https://simivalleylibrary.org/teen-advisory-group/

4. BRITE Youth
   Duties: Attend youth coalition meetings, participate in research projects, and collaborate with local government to address community concerns. Create and perform interactive workshops to address community issues like underage drinking and impaired driving.
   Qualifications: Open to teens entering the 7th through 12th grades who are interested in making their voices heard. Application can be found on their website.
   Website: https://www.briteyouth.org/bfi/

5. California Association of Student Councils
   Duties: Youth Advocacy: Act as a local and statewide voice for student views and lobby for youth-oriented legislation in Sacramento California Association of Student Councils.Per Training: Lead and facilitate workshops, retreats, and training conferences teaching skills like conflict resolution, meeting management, and project planning California Association of Student Councils. Proposal: Participate in statewide conferences to create and present formal proposals to the California State Board of Education or state legislative committees California Association of Student Councils.
   Qualifications: Open to high school students who are interested in student government, leadership, and advocacy. Must be willing to commit time to attend meetings, conferences, and events.State council and region cabinet positions usually require an application and a selection/interview process
   Website: https://www.casc.org/

6. Simi at the Garden
   Duties: Help out at monthly workdays and garden projects. Visit the website to register your interest in volunteering.
   Qualifications: None required.
   Website: https://www.simiatthegarden.org/contact-us

7. Simi Valley Historical Society
   Duties: Provide tours on Simi Valley history, help preserve historic items at Strathern Historical Park, or assist in the gift shop. Mon–Fri 9 AM–3 PM, Sat–Sun 1–4 PM.
   Qualifications: Junior Docent Program: Must be at least 12 years old and interested in the history of Simi Valley.
   Website: https://www.simihistory.com/

8. Simi Valley Public Library
   Duties: straightening books, prepping craft supplies, or working on special projects and assisting with events and programs as needed.
   Qualifications: 14+, friendly, have a high level of attention to detail and can work with minimal supervision.
   Website: https://www.simivalleypubliclibrary.org/

9. Simi Valley Youth Employment Service
   Duties: Capable of committing to a two-year term, attending monthly meetings (typically on the third Wednesday at 8:15 a.m. at City Hall). Advise the City Council on the specific expectations, needs, and challenges facing local youth in the job market.
   Qualifications: 16+, reside in Simi Valley
   Website: https://www.simivalleypubliclibrary.org/

10. Rancho Simi Recreation & Park District
   Duties: Assist with special events, office tasks, recreation classes, hiking trails, and preschool/afternoon programs.
   Qualifications: Must be at least 12 years old and complete an orientation available on the website.
   Website: https://www.rsrpd.org/

11. Simi Valley Police Explorers
   Duties: Trained in law enforcement areas, assist police at community events, and learn evidence collection and report writing.
   Qualifications: Must attend weekly meetings every Monday at 5:00 PM. Must be at least 14 years old or in 7th grade. Application required.
   Website: https://www.svpdexplorers.com/

12. Habitat for Humanity of Ventura County
   Duties: Help at the ReStore, serve as a photographer, or assist at special events. Sign-up required in advance.
   Qualifications: Ages 12–14 may volunteer with a parent/guardian. Must be 14+ to volunteer at ReStore.
   Website: https://habitatventura.org/volunteer/

13. The Salvation Army Simi Valley
   Duties: Prepare food bags during pantry hours for community members in need.
   Qualifications: None required. Just be ready to help wherever needed.
   Website: https://simivalley.salvationarmy.org/simi_valley_service_unit/

14. Ventura County Animal Services
   Duties: Animal care, fundraising, and helping staff with various tasks at the county shelter.
   Qualifications: Minimum age 13. Parent/guardian permission required. Must follow training protocols.
   Website: https://www.vcas.us/volunteer/

15. For the Need Foundation
   Duties: Attend volunteering events posted on the website. Duties vary depending on the event.
   Qualifications: Minimum age 13.
   Website: https://fortheneed.org/bowandarrow/

16. Thousand Oaks Library
   Duties: Various opportunities including events, teen volunteer programs, and more. Duties vary by event.
   Qualifications: Minimum age 13.
   Website: https://www.tolibrary.org/teens/teen-volunteers

17. Conejo Valley Historical Society
   Duties: Greet visitors, help in the office and gift shop, and assist with tours at the Stagecoach Inn Museum.
   Qualifications: Minimum age 10.
   Website: https://stagecoachinnmuseum.com/new-page-1

18. Moorpark Library
   Duties: Organize shelves, volunteer at events, give book recommendations, and other miscellaneous tasks as requested.
   Qualifications: Book recommendations: 6th grade+. Event volunteering and shelving: 7th grade+.
   Website: https://www.tolibrary.org/teens/teen-volunteers

19. For the Troops Foundation Simi Valley
   Duties: Write notes to soldiers, fill up care boxes, create flyers, and other supportive tasks for active-duty military.
   Qualifications: Minimum age 12.
   Website: https://www.forthetroops.org/

20. Children's Hunger Fund
   Duties: Pack resource boxes to give to churches that distribute them to families in need in the community.
   Qualifications: Must volunteer with a parent or guardian present.
   Website: https://volunteer.childrenshungerfund.org/opportunity/a1APH000001O09Z/volunteering-at-the-chf-distribution-center-in-sylmar-ca-fy2025

21. Simi Valley Youth Orchestra (SVYO)
   Duties: SVYO is all about helping young musicians grow — both as artists and as people. Inspiring a love of music, building lasting skills, and creating a space where discipline meets the joy of making music. An incredible journey of musical discovery, growth, and excellence.
   Qualifications: Must be 19 years old or younger and successfully audition for a position in the orchestra.
   Website: https://www.svyo.net/
